Black Soil (Regur Soil)

Black soil is one of the most important types of soil, primarily found in India and other tropical regions. It is known for its dark color and high fertility.

Characteristics

  • Color: Deep black to dark brown, due to its high clay content and iron-rich minerals.
  • Texture: Fine-grained and clayey.
  • Water Retention:
    • Retains moisture exceptionally well, making it suitable for dry farming.
    • Becomes sticky when wet and hardens when dry.
  • Fertility:
    • Rich in nutrients like calcium carbonate, magnesium, potash, and lime.
    • Poor in organic matter, phosphorus, and nitrogen.
  • Cracking: Develops deep cracks in summer, which helps in natural aeration of the soil.

Formation

Formed from the weathering of volcanic rocks, particularly basalt. Commonly found in regions with a semi-arid climate.

Locations

Majorly found in regions like the Deccan Plateau (India) in states such as Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh, and Karnataka.

Crops Grown

  • Well-suited for cotton cultivation, earning it the nickname "Cotton Soil".
  • Other crops:
    • Sorghum (jowar)
    • Millets
    • Sugarcane
    • Wheat
    • Groundnut
