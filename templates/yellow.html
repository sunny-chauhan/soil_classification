Yellow Soil

Yellow soil is a type of soil found in specific regions, known for its characteristic yellow color. Its color and properties are influenced by climatic conditions, the parent rock, and organic content.

Characteristics

  • Color:
    • Yellow, due to the presence of hydrated ferric oxide.
    • Changes to red under oxidation or intense sunlight.
  • Texture:
    • Fine to medium-grained.
    • May have a mix of sand, clay, and silt.
  • Fertility:
    • Low organic content and nutrients.
    • Requires fertilizers and soil treatment for agricultural use.
  • Water Retention: Moderate water-holding capacity.
  • pH: Generally acidic, depending on the region.

Formation

Formed by the weathering of crystalline and metamorphic rocks. Found in regions with heavy rainfall, where water leaches nutrients from the soil, leaving behind oxides of iron and aluminum.

Locations

Found in tropical and subtropical regions. In India:

  • Karnataka
  • Jharkhand
  • Odisha
  • Chhattisgarh
  • Parts of Tamil Nadu

Crops Grown

  • Suitable for:
    • Cotton
    • Millets
    • Pulses
    • Groundnut
    • Oilseeds
  • Crop growth improves with the addition of manures and fertilizers.
